I C B   A M C L   U N I T   F U N D


Formation

The Fund was created on 18 th May, 2003 by a Trust Deed executed between the sponsor and the Trustee under Trust Act, 1882 and Registration Act 1908. The Fund was registered by the Securities and Exchange Commission on 3 rd June, 2003 under the Securities and Exchange Commission (Mutual Fund) Rules, 2001. The Fund is an open ended Mutual Fund with a perpetual life. The initial target size was Tk. 100.00 million divided into 10,00,000 units of Tk. 100.00 each.


Sponsor

ICB Capital Management Ltd, a subsidiary of ICB is the sponsor of the Fund. The company was incorporated as a public limited company on 5 th December, 2000 under the Companies Act, 1994 with an authorized capital of Tk. 10.00 billion and a paid up capital of Tk. 8.2 billion. The Company obtained license from Securities and Exchange Commission on 16 th October, 2001 to carry out the merchant banking activities. The company is being managed by high calibered professional people mostly taken from ICB. The company subscribed units worth Tk. 10.00 million out of which10% i.e. Tk. 1.00 million shall be under the custody of the Trustee. These units are subject to lock in for the life of the Fund

Trustee & Custodian

Investment Corporation of Bangladesh (ICB) is the trustee and custodian of the fund. ICB was established on 1st October, 1976 under the Investment Corporation of Bangladesh Ordinance, 1976 (XL of 1976) with a view to encouraging and broadening the base of investment, develop the capital market, mobilize savings, promote & establish subsidiaries for business development and provide for matters ancillary thereto. ICB is the harbinger of mutual funds in the country. It was the single largest stock broker & dealer in the stock exchanges. ICB acted as an underwriter/manager to the issue to more than 370 companies of which 101 companies are publicly traded companies. ICB has acted as trustee to the debenture issues of over 15 companies amounting to Tk. 1.42 billion. ICB has been playing a unique role in the development of capital market of Bangladesh.
